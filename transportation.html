Transportation

Your guide to navigating Taniti City and the island's major attractions.

Public City Transit

Taniti City is served by a reliable public bus system operating daily from 5 a.m. to 11 p.m. It's an affordable and convenient way to explore the city.

Inter-Island Shuttles

For travel beyond Taniti City limits, several private bus companies provide routes across the island to key attractions. Tickets can be secured in advance or purchased at the local terminals.

Metered Taxi Service

Taxis are readily available throughout Taniti City and near popular attraction sites. Most drivers speak fluent English and conveniently accept both cash (USD) and major credit cards for payment.

Car Rentals

Visitors can rent a car near the airport for maximum travel flexibility. Taniti uses clear U.S.-style road signage, and all major international driver licenses are readily accepted for rental.

Check Availability

Bicycle Rentals

Numerous vendors offer affordable bike rentals across the island. Helmets are required by local law in all rentals. Taniti City is primarily flat, making it an excellent area for leisurely cycling.

Foot travel

Many visitors enjoy walking, particularly within Taniti City and the Merriton Landing area. The island's compact layout and scenic pedestrian paths make exploring comfortably on foot a popular option.

Arriving in Taniti

Taniti operates a small airport servicing propeller planes and smaller jet aircraft. Most international travelers arrive by air, though a cruise ship docks weekly in Yellow Leaf Bay for overnight stays, providing an alternate arrival route.
